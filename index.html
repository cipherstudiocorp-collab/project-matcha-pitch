The practice

A matcha bar,
built on ritual.

Project Matcha began with a question: if Portland has three coffee shops on every block, why not one place that takes tea this seriously? Josh Pak and Purin Moungvang opened the doors in the spring of 2025 with a single practice — stone chawan, matcha sieved before use, one cup whisked at a time.

The drinks list has since grown. The ritual has not. On quiet afternoons and on the days when the line curls around the corner, every cup is prepared the same way — slowly, by hand, by someone who cares about what ends up in it.

The practice

One cup,
poured by hand.

Matcha sieved, whisked in a stone chawan, layered over cold milk in house cups. Every pour happens the same way — unhurried, in front of you. The drinks change with the seasons; the method does not.

The list

A small menu,
prepared deliberately.

Seasonal drinks rotate through the year. The classics don't. Every cup begins with ceremonial grade matcha from Uji, whisked to order.

  1. 01 The Project Matcha Ceremonial matcha, bergamot-steeped milk — the drink we're named for.
  2. 02 Double Matcha Two pulls of ceremonial grade, no milk. The honest one.
  3. 03 Coconut Matcha Cloud Cold coconut cream, matcha whisked through, finished with whipped.
  4. 04 The Strawberry Matcha Whole Oregon strawberries, cold milk, ceremonial matcha on top.
  5. 05 Raspberry Vanilla Matcha House-made raspberry, bourbon vanilla, cold milk, matcha.
  6. 06 Banana Cream Matcha Fresh banana, oat milk, ceremonial matcha, soft whip.
  7. 07 Yuzu Matcha Tonic Citrus tonic, fresh yuzu peel, matcha — the summer drink.
  8. 08 Matcha Einspänner Double matcha, Viennese-style dense cream on top. Sip through the cream.

The shop  ·  555 NE Couch Street

Two friends

Josh & Purin.

Josh Pak and Purin Moungvang grew up drinking matcha at home and couldn't find it prepared properly anywhere in the city. What started as a weekend habit — whisking bowls in a borrowed kitchen, inviting friends over — turned into a storefront off Couch Street. They're still the ones behind the counter on most mornings.

“We treat every cup the same way, whether there are two of you in the shop or a line out the door. That's the part we refuse to change.”
— Josh Pak, Co-founder
